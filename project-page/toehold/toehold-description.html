Toehold

The toehold can be triggered by short single stranded DNA fragments. We have chosen two different sizes for the triggering fragments: a short trigger, which is exactly 36nt long and complementary to the toehold in its entirety, and a long trigger, which is 90nt long, contains the region complementary to the toehold in its middle and flanking sequences before and after.

Furthermore, the toehold can be triggered by RNA fragments.

Additionally, our chosen aptamer is able to trigger the switch as well.

This is useful as the lacZalpha subunit is much smaller and requires thus less ressources to be expressed.
