LacZ expression in all lysates

Having chosen ß-Galactosidase as the main reporter protein, the choice for the lysate that we are using had to be made based on the respective expression of the reporter in each protein.

BL21 was not a good choice as the fully functioning enzyme was already present in the lysate and an immediate colour change could be observed. The BL21 lysate based on YTPG medium started on a lower expression level than the normal BL21 lysate. This is most probably due to the different pathways of metabolism. In YTPG, the enzyme ß-Galactosidase is not used for the general metabolism, so its expression is much lower in the beginning.

The expression levels of Top10-GamS lysate are very unexpected and most likely due to overspill or pipetting errors during the preparation of the experiment.

Both M15-T7 lysates reach a satisfying level of ß-Galactosidase expression that produces a colour change visible for the naked eye. The M15-T7 lysate is thus our lysate of choice.
