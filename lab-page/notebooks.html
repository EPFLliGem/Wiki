10/07/2017-16/07/2017
This week was all about moving into our new lab and getting everything organised!
  • We started running PCRs to amplify linear DNA templates we needed for our experiments.
  • To see whether our lysates are able to express β-Galactosidase, we run plate reader experiment that took a bit longer than anticipated, because the plate reader wasn't working in the beginning.
  • We created our own competent cells and transformed them with a vector containing part of the β-Gal gene that can be found on the iGEM plates.
  • Worked on fabrication of MITOMI chips that can be attached to epoxy-coated slides.
  • Worked on fabrication of 50 epoxy-coated glass slides for our experiments.
  • Four of the epoxy-coated glass slides were spotted with Cy3/Cy5-labeled diluted aptamers.
  • An experiment was carried out using previously fabricated MITOMI chips to determine wheter or not the thrombin aptamers TBA (G15D) and HD22 (60-18[29]a) are able to bind biotinylated-thrombin. As expected, we observe a fluorescent signal from Cy3/Cy5 labeled anti-thrombin aptamers bound to immobilized biotinylated thrombin in the button areas. This experiment was repeated three times and a calibration curve for both Cy3 and Cy5 on the same MITOMI chip was performed.
